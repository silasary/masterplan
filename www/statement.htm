A statement on Decompilation

25th Feburary, 2017

History

  • The last official update (11.11) for Masterplan was in 2013, and contained several major usability issues.
  • The original domain, http://masterplan.habitualindolence.net/ expired in March 2016.
  • After enduring a number of frustrating and fatal crashes, I attempted to contact Andy through all available channels, with no luck.

Why decompile Masterplan?

The last official release, 11.11, has several breaking issues, which have been slowly getting on my nerves over the past several years.
These included random crashes, and major memory leaks.
At first, I used ILSpy as an attempt to identify and avoid the crashes I'd been experiencing.
Noticing several glaring errors (including 115+ points where a memory leak would occur), I decompiled it and fixed them for personal use

Why put it online?

A couple weeks ago, I mentioned that I'd been cleaning it up to a couple of friends in my local GM group.
They asked for a copy, having also noticed the same shortcomings, and one of them put that build online.
As such, I decided that, as I was already mirroring 11.11, I may as well start putting my 11.12 build up here
I figured that, if Andy Aiken is still alive, and really doesn't want me messing with Masterplan, he would be able to contact me and ask me to take it down

So what now?

I'm still trying to work out how the codebase was put together, and it's currently full of all kinds of decompilation junk
I'm going to continue to familiarize myself with it, then start adding features based on my own personal feature wishlist.
